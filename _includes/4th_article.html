Starting Academic Routine: Week One at University
1. Orientation and Lectures Begin

The second week marked the beginning of official classes. We received our textbooks, schedules, and initial lectures. The workload was heavy, and understanding the timetable was initially confusing. Each professor introduced their subject, and we had to adjust to their teaching styles quickly. I realized that attending every lecture, despite confusion or fatigue, was essential to keeping up with the curriculum. It was an intense start, but necessary for building a strong academic foundation.

2. Weekend Relief and Travel Challenges

At the end of the week, we returned home for the weekend. Traveling was challenging due to limited transport and long distances. The journey took time, but the rest and home environment helped me recharge. During this period, I reflected on the first week’s experiences and prepared mentally for the upcoming week. Managing travel and study was demanding, but it taught me planning, patience, and self-discipline.

3. Settling into Routine

Returning from the weekend, we faced more adjustments. The timetable had minor changes, and I had to coordinate with roommates and friends. Slowly, I developed a routine of attending lectures, completing assignments, and socializing responsibly. By balancing academic and personal life, I felt more confident navigating the campus. The first full week of structured learning taught me time management, perseverance, and resilience in the face of minor disruptions.
