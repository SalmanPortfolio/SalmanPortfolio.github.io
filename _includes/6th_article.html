Facing Academic Challenges and Midterm Pressure

As I moved into the sixth and seventh weeks of my academic journey, things started becoming more serious and challenging. This was the time when our studies became more intense, and we began facing quizzes and midterm exams. Initially, I was not fully prepared for the quiz pattern, and I had no clear idea about how questions would be asked or what type of preparation was required. Because of this confusion, I faced many difficulties during my first few quizzes.

1. Struggles with Quiz Patterns

During the early quizzes, I realized that university-level assessments were very different from what I had experienced before. The questions were more conceptual and required deeper understanding rather than simple memorization. Since I was not aware of the pattern, I couldn’t perform well. This created stress and made me realize that I needed to change my study approach. It was a tough learning phase where I had to adapt quickly.

2. Midterm Preparation Challenges

After the quizzes, the midterm exams started approaching. This period was very stressful because I still did not fully understand how to prepare effectively. Coming from a pre-medical background, subjects like mathematics and programming were already difficult for me. I had never studied these subjects in depth before, so everything felt new and complicated. I spent long hours trying to understand concepts, but sometimes it felt like I was not making enough progress.

During midterm preparation, I often stayed up late at night, trying to complete my syllabus. Despite my efforts, I was unable to fully cover all the topics. There was always something left incomplete, which made me anxious. I tried my best to manage time, but balancing multiple subjects was not easy at all. This period taught me how important planning and consistency are in academic life.

3. Learning from Difficult Experiences

When the midterm exams finally started, I faced several challenges. Some papers did not go as expected because I was still struggling with understanding the concepts clearly. It was disappointing, but at the same time, it became a valuable learning experience for me. I realized that hard work alone is not enough; smart work and proper guidance are equally important.

Even though my midterm performance was not very good, it helped me understand my weaknesses. I learned that I needed to focus more on understanding concepts rather than just trying to memorize things. This phase also made me mentally stronger and prepared me for future challenges.

Overall, the sixth and seventh weeks of my journey were full of difficulties, but they played a crucial role in shaping my academic mindset. These challenges pushed me to improve, adapt, and become more serious about my studies. Although it was a tough time, it became an important step in my learning journey.
