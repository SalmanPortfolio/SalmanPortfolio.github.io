Struggles and Learning: Quizzes, Meds, and New Challenges
1. Facing Quizzes for the First Time

During the sixth week at university, our first set of quizzes started. Coming from a Pre-Medical background, I was unprepared for the patterns and types of questions in these quizzes. Each quiz posed unexpected challenges, and I often felt confused about how to approach the problems. The pressure of time and the unfamiliar format added to the difficulty, making me realize how different engineering studies were compared to my previous academic experience.

2. Meds Preparation and Struggles

After the fourth week, our mid-term exams (Meds) began. Preparation for Meds required focused study, but much of the material was unfamiliar to me. Two weeks passed quickly as I tried to cover the syllabus, yet the pace of the course and the volume of topics made it difficult to fully grasp concepts. Night after night, I spent long hours studying, but often found that I could not cover all the required topics. The experience taught me resilience and the importance of consistent practice and planning, even when the workload seemed overwhelming.

3. Overcoming Academic Gaps

The sixth and seventh weeks highlighted the gaps in my foundational knowledge. Math and programming topics were particularly challenging because my background was in Pre-Medical studies. Despite these difficulties, I tried to adapt by seeking help from peers, revisiting lectures, and practicing coding exercises. Although the process was frustrating and exhausting, it strengthened my problem-solving skills and determination. By the end of the week, I gained confidence in handling engineering-specific challenges, realizing that perseverance and hard work are key to bridging knowledge gaps.

Throughout this period, I learned that transitioning from Pre-Medical to Engineering was not easy. Every quiz, assignment, and Meds preparation session highlighted the need for strategic study methods and active engagement. The challenges were intense, but they provided valuable lessons about time management, self-discipline, and seeking support when needed. This week reinforced the idea that struggles are part of the learning process and that persistence can turn obstacles into growth opportunities.
