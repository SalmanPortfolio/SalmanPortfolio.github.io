Adapting and Social Learning in the Third Week
1. Friendships and Peer Guidance

By the third week, I had formed bonds with classmates and hostel roommates. One of my friends, Bilal, became a key guide. He helped me understand social expectations and campus rules. We also faced senior-related challenges, including tasks that tested patience and creativity. Bilal and I learned together, supporting each other to navigate difficult situations. This highlighted the importance of teamwork and social learning in university life.

2. Experiencing Senior Challenges

During this period, seniors organized events and required us to participate in creative tasks. One challenge involved demonstrating a skill—dance in our case—which was a mix of entertainment and evaluation. While initially stressful, this activity became a way to build confidence, learn presentation skills, and reduce hesitation in social settings. I realized that such experiences, though unconventional, contribute to personal growth and adaptation in a new environment.

3. Maintaining Academic Focus

Despite social and senior-related distractions, I gradually returned to focusing on regular studies. Lectures and assignments demanded attention, and balancing social life with academics became a critical skill. By the end of the third week, I had developed strategies for time management, maintaining focus, and handling stress. This period reinforced resilience, adaptability, and self-discipline—key skills that would continue to shape my university journey in the months ahead.
